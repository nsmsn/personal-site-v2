Mastodon servers

Published November 26, 2022 by Nick
Edited on Dec. 6, 2022: I recently moved to a smaller instance. Mastodon.design is a well-moderated instance of a few hundred people, and may be more “on brand” for me.

I created an account on Mastodon back in April 2017, mainly to claim my dumb five-character username. Now that many of the people (web developers, artists, academics, media people) I once followed on Twitter are migrating to the open-source, federated social network, I may find myself using this account more. Update: You can now find me at @nsmsn@mastodon.design

I still plan to use my Micro.blog for most of my microblogging, and will try to keep my /following page on this website up to date. Generally, I’m interested in working on connecting the microblog to my main website and syndicating content to my other social channels. The websites of Jeremy Keith and Paul Robert Lloyd are two of the best examples I’ve encountered.

Finding people and their servers on Mastodon #

Amelia Wattenberger made a handy web app for finding people from Twitter with Mastodon accounts. The app organizes the people you follow based on the Mastodon instance their account is on. Earlier this week, I used this app to find and re-follow several people who’ve retired their Twitter accounts and moved to Mastodon in a large exodus.

This is not an exhaustive list of Mastodon servers, just a list (in late November 2022) where some the people I follow landed in the “fediverse”.
